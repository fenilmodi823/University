About Me

I’m a sixth‑semester Computer Engineering student at Silver Oak University, where I’ve built a solid foundation in Python, C++, and Java through both coursework and hands‑on projects.

My passion lies at the intersection of aerospace technology and software: I’m currently developing a Python‑based ISS trajectory tracker called the Space Debris Tracking System using NASA’s public APIs. Alongside that, I’ve built a Flappy Bird clone, created an air quality detector, developed a WhatsApp messaging app, worked on ISS detection and tracking, and explored fractal generation with the Mandelbrot equation. I’m also exploring AI applications in aerospace. A problem‑solver and team‑player by nature, I’m driven to bring innovative science‑communication projects to life—whether that’s pioneering a Global Science Explorer brand or collaborating on open‑source tools that make space data more accessible.
